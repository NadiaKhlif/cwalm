CWALM: A Lexical Corpus-based Model of Contemporary Written Arabic

This project proposes a new approach to analyze the Arabic language using corpus linguistics. The main objective is to study contemporary written Arabic through a representative corpus that is defined by objective criteria such as time, gender, and domain. The aim is to create a lexical resource that presents Modern Standard Arabic and its dialect variations as a complex and unified entity, by objectively describing written Arabic.

CWALM will provide the scientific community of Social Sciences and Humanities with:

Our Goals
  • New theoretical approach to overcome the traditional description of Arabic linguistic system.
  • Final test model that aims to be the first large-scale validated CWA resource.
  • Lexicographic resource that could have a positive social impact on Arabic-speaking communities.

Corpus

We collected corpus data from various social media platforms to encompass a wide range of user-generated content. This corpus includes data from both Tunisian and Moroccan dialects, ensuring comprehensive coverage of Colloquial Arabic Varieties.
